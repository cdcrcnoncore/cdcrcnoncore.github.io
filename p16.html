How did you get to know about opportunity?

On campus


What is your Job Profile?

Data Science Intern


Can you give a general discription of the interview with some interview questions?

Online round: On Hackerrank, About 15 MCQs and 3 codes only in Python. Duration was 1 hour. MCQs consisted of code snippet outputs or bugs and problems on probability. I did not perform well in this round as I did not have in depth knowledge of Python, I mostly program in other languages. Problems were not difficult.
Interview: 2 interviewers, duration was about 30 minutes. Interview revolved around my projects and resume. They mostly asked about my ML related projects although I had listed others too. Asked about my experience with Python, git, linux and ML. I told I was most comfortable with C++ and Java, but coding in Python is not an issue, have good experience with git, linux.
What metrics are used for measuring performance of a predictive model? I talked about precision and recall, but I got a little confused between the two on a cross question. Was asked if I knew about f-1 score, which I didn't. They also asked about the application of different types of deep learning models, to which I answered by telling about some different neural networks and the domains where they are mostly used. I talked about the 2 projects I had done in computer vision with pytorch, I was asked why pytorch, to which I replied that it is a new DL library which is widely used in academia and offers good performance and functionality. Also talked about how it also supports Cuda which makes training of models much faster.
Why are you interested in this internship and profile?
I told that this was a very interesting profile to me as it was a balance of two things I like, working on latest technologies and business, finance. I was asked if I follow stocks, to which I said yes but not very regularly. Talked a bit about what Nifty and other indices are. I asked questions about the type of projects in this internship and the current work from home situation.


How did you prepare for it?

Personally, I did not really prepare for this job description. I was more focused on preparing for SDE profiles, getting selected here came as a surprise. But, I would suggest to brush up your combinatorics and probability which I did for two days before the first round. brilliant.org is an excellent website for this purpose. Experience with Python will also help, I was not much aware of things such as lambda expressions, generators in Python.


Any tips for juniors?

For this or a similar profile, get your basics crystal clear in Python programming. Solve problems for a few days on probability distributions. Have ML related projects on your resume and talk to the interviewers like in a conversation, so you can talk more about your strong points.
